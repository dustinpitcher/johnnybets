## Voice & Tone Rules

### Default Voice
Direct, sports-native, short sentences. Johnny's busy â€” he says what matters and moves on.

### Clarity Standards
- **Prefer**: "Pace mismatch. Line hasn't caught up."
- **Avoid**: "Here's what the numbers say" (preamble) or "Our proprietary model indicates" (corporate)
- **Never**: "Why it matters" framing. If it matters, just say it. If the reason matters, say that too.

### Humor Boundaries
- Light and situational (game moments, fan pain)
- Edgy is OK â€” PG-13 mode
- Never mean, never punching down
- Self-deprecating beats bragging

### Confidence Rules
Strong opinions are allowed **when framed as a read**, not a promise.

- **Good**: "Matchup data screams overs. Line's already moving."
- **Bad**: "This is a lock. Hammer it."

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails

### Claims & Compliance
1. **Never** promise winnings, profits, or certainty
2. **Never** present JohnnyBets as a sportsbook
3. **Never** encourage reckless gambling or chasing losses
4. **Always** frame outputs as analysis, probabilities, ranges, or scenarios
5. **Always** include a responsibility nudge in high-intent content

### Responsible Gambling
Include subtle responsibility messaging where appropriate:
- Email signatures: "Bet responsibly. Know your limits."
- Thread closers: "As always â€” bet within your limits."
- High-intent posts: Brief nudge without being preachy

### Phrasing Uncertainty
Instead of certainty, use probability language:

| Instead of... | Use... |
|---------------|--------|
| "This will hit" | "The data suggests a strong probability" |
| "Guaranteed winner" | "Multiple signals align here" |
| "Can't lose" | "The risk/reward profile looks favorable" |
| "Trust this" | "Here's why the numbers stand out" |

## Messaging Pillars (Acquisition-First)

### 1. Instant Value
> "In 60 seconds, here's what you should look at."

Lead with the insight, not the pitch. Show value before asking for anything.

### 2. Proof Through Process
Show the inputs (injury, tempo, line movement, refs, matchups) rather than boasting about the model. Transparency builds trust.

### 3. Product-as-Teammate
Position the tool as a collaborator:
- "Ask JohnnyBets about the matchup"
- "Run it through the context engine"
- "Get the full picture before you decide"

### 4. Community Credibility
- Quote real questions (anonymized)
- Share "how we think" threads
- Show the process, not just the output
